ABOUT

How I got here.

I started coding thirteen years ago out of pure curiosity, and that curiosity turned into a career. Years of hands-on engineering across Microsoft 365, Azure, React, and AWS, enough to be recognized as a Microsoft MVP along the way.

Along the way I started mentoring other engineers on the technical side, and I never stopped. I've always believed you don't really understand something until you can teach it to someone else, so teaching became the way I check my own thinking.

Somewhere in there I moved from writing code to leading the people who write it. Then I made a more deliberate move, from engineering leadership into product. The reason was simple: the biggest impact I could have wasn't in how something got built, it was in deciding what to build and why.

Today I lead the Growth Product part of Rebrandly. I own the roadmap from discovery to delivery, run the experiments, and stay close enough to the code to debug a webhook when it matters. I can have a strategy conversation with stakeholders in the morning and dig into an architecture problem with engineering in the afternoon, and that dual fluency is the part I like most. It lets me decide faster and lose less between product and engineering.

That same instinct came with me into the AI era. As I go deeper into building agentic AI systems, I teach what I learn while I'm still learning it, and I trade product and growth knowledge with the people around me. Teaching it is still how I make sure I really know it.
